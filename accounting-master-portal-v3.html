Debit/Credit logic (mapped to A/L/E):
• Debits: Assets ↑, Expenses ↑ (Equity ↓), Dividends ↑ (Equity ↓), Liabilities ↓, Equity ↓, Revenue ↓ (Equity ↓)
• Credits: Assets ↓, Expenses ↓ (Equity ↑), Dividends ↓ (Equity ↑), Liabilities ↑, Equity ↑, Revenue ↑ (Equity ↑)

Objective 1 — Nature of Business & Accounting

Business
An organization that transforms inputs (resources) into outputs (goods/services) to customers.
Profit
The difference between amounts received from customers and amounts paid for inputs.
Profit = Revenues − Expenses
Tip: include both Revenues and Expenses.

Objective 2 — GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)

GAAP is the guideline framework used when preparing financial statements.

Assumptions
Monetary unit • Time period • Business entity • Going concern
Principles
Measurement • Historical cost • Revenue recognition • Expense recognition (matching)

Objective 5 — Financial Statements

The primary financial statements of a corporation (in order prepared):

1
Income Statement — revenues and expenses for a period of time.
2
Retained Earnings Statement — changes in retained earnings during a period.
3
Balance Sheet — assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific date.
4
Statement of Cash Flows — cash receipts/payments for a period; operating, investing, financing.

Chapter Summary

3
Accounting equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity.
